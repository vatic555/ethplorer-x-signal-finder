# Opportunity Gate

## Purpose

Strictly decide whether a candidate Signal contains a real information gap that a specific, documented Ethplorer asset or capability can naturally and credibly close.

Do not search for a way to mention Ethplorer. Search for an existing information gap.

## Required Inputs

- Candidate Signal and all linked source-post evidence
- Available thread, reply, quote-post, or external context
- Reviewed knowledge-base asset records and terminology
- Supported product, network, and non-goal boundaries

## Required Outputs

Return structured data containing:

- `decision`: `accepted`, `rejected`, or `unresolved`
- the exact information gap stated independently of Ethplorer
- answers and evidence for every Gate question
- the exact Ethplorer asset or capability required, including its knowledge-base identifier
- audience benefit and natural-relevance rationale
- forced-promotion assessment
- verified facts, model inferences, unresolved uncertainties, and needed context
- concise decision rationale

## Rejection Behavior

Reject generic commentary. Reject forced marketing relevance. Reject a candidate whose only value is that Ethplorer can be mentioned. Reject when Ethplorer has no specific advantage over a generic crypto commentator. Do not generate a draft for rejected Signals. Do not generate a draft at this stage for any decision.

Mark the candidate `unresolved` if the knowledge base does not support the proposed contribution, if the exact asset cannot be identified, or if material facts require more context. Unresolved Signals must not become Opportunities or receive drafts.

## Evidence and Uncertainty Rules

Every Gate answer must cite specific input or knowledge-base evidence. Never invent a capability or treat a model inference as verified fact. Conflicting claims must remain visible. Accept only when the facts and documented asset are sufficient for a credible, audience-helpful contribution. Prefer rejection or unresolved status over a weak acceptance.
